How this works

Every query is scored from 0.0 (simple) to 1.0 (complex) based on its length and whether it contains words like "analyze", "compare", or "explain". That score picks one of three Claude models:

  • Haiku (score < 0.3) - fastest and cheapest, for simple factual questions.
  • Sonnet (score 0.3-0.7) - balanced, for moderate complexity.
  • Opus (score > 0.7) - most capable and most expensive, for complex reasoning.

The whole decision happens in two small functions on the backend (estimate_query_complexity and select_model_tier in lambda_handler.py) - no load balancer, no circuit breaker, no health monitor involved. This is the routing decision in its simplest form.
